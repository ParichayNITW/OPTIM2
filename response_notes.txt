This repository contains optimization logic that depends on runtime data and solver configuration. Producing hour-by-hour numeric outputs outside the application requires the full solver execution and intermediate state traces, which are not derived analytically from the JSON example alone. Use the application's reporting utilities or dedicated tracing to obtain exact numbers for specific hourly scenarios.
DP candidate audit logs show queue_km as the treated-length commitment, which can exceed the physical line length when upstream segments are still carrying chemical from prior injections; the total reflects in-line plus upstream commitments preserved across stations.

Detailed step example (Paradip → Balasore → Haldia)
- Line length: 328 km (Paradip 158 km + Balasore 170 km). Manual floor: 4 ppm over the whole 328 km. Upstream carryover: 14 km of 6 ppm from the prior hour/day still approaching Paradip.
- Initial queue at 07:00: [14 km @ 6 ppm] + [158 km @ 4 ppm] + [170 km @ 4 ppm] = 342 km total. This is why the first DP log row can show queue_km = 342 km even though the steel is 328 km; the extra 14 km is the treated fluid that has not yet entered Paradip.
- Paradip candidate (example): choose Pump B at 2,400 rpm with 0 ppm extra DRA. Outlet head after friction/elevation leaves 190 m residual (enough above min). Queue advancement for the 158 km segment: consume 14 km of 6 ppm and 144 km of the 4 ppm floor, leaving [14 km @ 4 ppm] from the original floor’s second segment. Paradip injects 8 ppm for 30 km to refresh the front. New queue handed to Balasore: [30 km @ 8 ppm] + [170 km @ 4 ppm] = 200 km, so the Balasore log may show queue_km ≈ 200 km for that candidate.
- Balasore candidate (example): run its pump at 2,900 rpm with no extra DRA. After pumping its 170 km stretch, the queue advances by 170 km: first 30 km @ 8 ppm consumed, then 140 km of the 4 ppm segment consumed, leaving 30 km @ 4 ppm toward Haldia. If Balasore adds 6 ppm for 20 km, the outbound queue becomes [20 km @ 6 ppm] + [30 km @ 4 ppm] = 50 km when reaching the terminal.
- Residual and protected in the log: each candidate row records the station’s computed residual head (e.g., 190 m after Paradip, 120 m after Balasore) and whether it was protected (true when using 0 ppm or the station’s minimum rpm), so you can see which options survived pruning and why.
